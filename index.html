Let's talk about:
Animal Crossing!

As an unlimited life simulation, the Animal Crossing series does not have a set goal - as such, after an introductory sequence at the beginning of the games, players are free to do whatever they want, within the possibilities offered. . The only "target" in the game, if any, is to pay the mortgage on the player's house. But, like most things in the game, this is entirely optional and incurs no consequences other than having less space to store items.

The game does not have a linear structure, in the sense that it has a beginning, a middle, but no end. The player takes the role of a human being in a town inhabited by animals and can perform tasks such as paying his mortgage, taking care of the garden, fishing, catching insects, among others.

Dōbutsu no Mori

Dōbutsu no Mori (どうぶつの森), translated as Animal Forest, is the first game in the Animal Crossing saga. This game was only released in Japan. The concept of the game is that a player moves to a town where all his neighbors are animals and each one has a different personality.

The game was released exclusively for Japan on April 14, 2001 for the Nintendo 64.

Its initial price was ¥5,800 (equivalent to 47 euros or 53 dollars). Nintendo also launched a special pack that included a controller for the console, this version went on sale for ¥6,800 (equivalent to 55 euros or 62 dollars).
